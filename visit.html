IPL 2021

The 2021 Indian Premier Tournament was the fourteenth season of the Indian Premier League (IPL), a professional Twenty20 cricket league created by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in 2007. It was also known as IPL 14 or, for sponsorship reasons, Vivo IPL 2021. The Mumbai Indians were the reigning champions for the second year in a row, having won both the 2019 and 2020 seasons.The BCCI initially announced the tournament's fixtures on March 7, 2021. After an increase in COVID-19 instances within the bio bubbles of individual teams, the event was postponed indefinitely on 4 May 2021. Thirty-one of the scheduled sixty matches were still to be played at the time of the suspension. The BCCI confirmed on May 29, 2021 that the tournament's final matches will be held in the United Arab Emirates in September and October 2021. On July 25, 2021, the schedule for the remainder of the tournament was published.The BCCI said on January 30, 2021 that they were confident in their ability to host the event in India. They also stated that the UAE was not being considered as a backup venue.The BCCI also declared on the day of the auction that Vivo has returned as the title sponsor after withdrawing from the last event.The BCCI released the entire schedule for the season on March 7, 2021. Matches were planned at six locations, including all five chosen sites as well as the added option of Mumbai. No team was scheduled to play at their home location to avoid home advantage. The season was set to begin on April 9 and culminate on May 30 at the Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad with the final. The BCCI also announced that the competition will start behind closed doors, with a decision on whether or not spectators would be let in to be made later.   Hyderabad was added as a backup location after the unexpected surge of COVID-19 instances in India, albeit no matches were played there.If the local government allowed it, BCCI wanted to enable at least 50% of the stadium's crowd to get vaccinated after the move to the UAE. Spectators were permitted in four days before the restart by following COVID-19 regulations. [COVID-19 IMPACT] Several players withdrew from the competition because to the growing COVID-19 scenario in India. Ravichandran Ashwin left the Delhi Capitals bubble after the Chennai leg of the competition to "help [his] family" amid the epidemic. After two Kolkata Knight Riders players tested positive for COVID-19 on May 3, 2021, the planned match between Kolkata Knight Riders and Royal Challengers Bangalore was postponed. Three members of the Chennai Super Kings camp tested positive on the same day, including bowling coach Lakshmipathy Balaji and CEO Kasi Viswanathan. The Delhi Capitals' whole team was quarantined later that day. Similarly, before of their encounter on May 4, the Mumbai Indians and Sunrisers Hyderabad missed their practise sessions.
